About Me

Who I Am

I'm a computational engineer and PhD researcher at the von Karman Institute for Fluid Dynamics (Brussels, Belgium), working on advanced reactive-flow solvers for Rotating Detonation Engine (RDE) simulations. My work lives at the intersection of high-fidelity CFD, large-scale HPC, and machine learning.

I build and validate numerical solvers from the governing equations up — coupling OpenFOAM with Cantera for detailed finite-rate chemistry — and run multi-million-cell simulations on AMD EPYC clusters. I also apply neural networks and PINNs to accelerate simulation workflows.

Before my PhD, I worked across Germany, the UK, and India — at Dassault Systèmes, Helmholtz-Zentrum, Jaguar Land Rover, Tata Technologies, and Suzuki — bringing both research depth and real-world engineering perspective.
