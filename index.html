01 · About

From governing equations
to running solvers.

Brussels, BE · 50.85°N

I'm a computational engineer and PhD researcher at the von Kármán Institute for Fluid Dynamics, working on advanced reactive-flow solvers for Rotating Detonation Engine simulation. My work sits at the intersection of high-fidelity CFD, large-scale HPC, and machine learning.

I build and validate numerical solvers from the governing equations up — coupling OpenFOAM with Cantera for detailed finite-rate chemistry — then run multi-million-cell simulations on AMD EPYC clusters and mine the results with custom C++ analysis modules. On the side, I apply neural networks and PINNs to accelerate simulation workflows.

Before the PhD, I spent a decade in industry and research across Germany, the UK, and India — Dassault Systèmes, Helmholtz-Zentrum Dresden-Rossendorf, Jaguar Land Rover, Tata Technologies, and Suzuki — so I bring both research depth and shipped-product engineering judgment.
